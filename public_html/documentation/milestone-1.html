Purpose, Audience & Goal

Purpose:

  • This will be a professional portfolio website that list the skills and knowledge I have learned from the Deep Dive Bootcamp.

Audience:

  • A prime audience would be different corporations and fellow IT Professionals, specifically within the computer programming fields. Can also include, any recruiters, out-of-state corporations and recruiters, hiring/project managers. Also, for fellow peers who could use any advice or input.

Goal:

  • The overall goal is a site for companies and fellow peers to be able to easily contact me with any opportunities for employment or to simply reach out for help on any specific topics from the skills I have acquired from my experience. Also, as a way to reference all my completed work, skills and knowledge I have acquired, and to make more content available to reference my online presence.

Persona

Name: Paul Johnson

Age: 35


Profession

  • Paul is a project manager for a small company based in Seattle. Paul is looking web developer to add to his team. After bad experiences with previous hires, he has decided to take an active role in finding the “right” candidate. Paul is all about finding the skills and likes to see any work-related experience of a perspective candidate.

Technology

  • Uses and is efficient with both A Macbook Pro and HP laptop with windows 10. Has a complete home office with an LG 45’ Smart TV connected to his laptops. Uses an Iphone 11 Pro max with a 15’ touch screen that he uses to keep track of work through-out the day.

Attitudes and Behaviors

  • As a program manager, Paul is always on the look-out for new developers and team members. He is always loaded with different projects so keeps a very busy schedule and often takes work home with him. Paul likes to see actual work to compare if the skills are in line with the projects details. Often asks fellow peers and recruiters for potential team-members.

Frustrations and Needs

  • Paul is running short on time and within 3 weeks needs to hire someone for a specific project that is being worked on. Paul needs to find someone who is a team player. He has multiple projects coming up and needs to find perspective candidates to fill the positions. Paul is worried that he will not be able to find the right candidate with the specific skills and knowledge on a specific project. Pay is extremely competitive, and Paul is willing to hire less-experienced developers with a great form of reference for all their skills and knowledge.

Goals

  • Paul is focused on one thing: To hire any protentional candidates, experienced or with little experience. To also, analyze the skills, knowledge and portfolio of any completed projects, based on their online presence. Too than decide if the skills and knowledge would be an asset to the team and the project.

User Story

  • As a program manager, I need to analyze based on skills and work whether this person is a good candidate for a specific project.
